Integrity

A physics engine where the physics is real — no fudge, at any level.

Integrity is an experiment: build a browser physics engine from the matter up, model real materials faithfully, and let the laws of physics emerge — never script them.

Most engines fake it. A crate falls because someone wrote "make crates fall"; a wall shatters because a designer authored the pieces. Integrity refuses that. There is one rule — real matter with real properties, integrated honestly through time — and everything else is a consequence: an object falls because it is unsupported; rock breaks only when stress exceeds its strength; a dropped Moon shatters because the impact energy dwarfs its binding energy.

  • Emergent, not scripted. Kepler orbits, escape velocity, the angle a pile of sand holds, crater scaling — all fall out of particle physics, none are coded as rules.
  • Faithful to declared physics. The parameters (G, a planet's mass, a material's density and strength) declare real physics. The engine's job is to honour them exactly — not to approximate for convenience.
  • A learning tool, not just a game engine. If the physics is honest, you can re-derive it by playing — a child (and, one day, an embodied AI) could discover the laws of nature from inside the simulation. A fudged engine teaches wrong physics.
