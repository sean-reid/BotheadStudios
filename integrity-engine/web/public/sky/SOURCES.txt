WHY 3-D POSITIONS AND NOT A SKY SPHERE
  A sphere of stars painted at "infinity" is a Sol-centric universe: it silently asserts that every
  star is equidistant and that the observer is always here. It is wrong the moment anything moves far
  enough to matter, and it cannot survive interstellar travel at all -- the shell would simply drag
  along with the camera and the constellations would never change.
  So the file carries each star's real CARTESIAN POSITION and its ABSOLUTE magnitude. The engine
  computes direction and brightness from the actual geometry every frame: direction from
  (star - observer), brightness from the inverse-square law over that true distance. From here that
  reproduces the sky we see -- because it IS the sky we see. From Sirius it would show Sirius's sky,
  with Sol among the stars, for free and with no extra code.

HOW THIS FILE WAS MADE (from hyg_v44.csv.gz, sha256 00b349893b9a53106dd488d8371e8d2fa586043e500bb3cdb8bff3931682197d)
  * dropped HYG id 0 — that row is the Sun, which the engine renders as the real body it is
  * kept apparent magnitude <= 6.5, the naked-eye limit
  * dropped 205 stars whose parallax gives no usable distance (HYG marks these dist = 100000). A star
    with no distance has no position, and guessing one would be inventing a universe. 8,715 remain.
  * converted HYG's axes to the engine's: HYG has X -> vernal equinox, Y -> RA 6h, Z -> north celestial
    pole; the engine (crate::geo) has X -> RA 0, Y -> north pole, Z -> -(RA 6h). So (x, z, -y).
  * sorted by apparent magnitude AS SEEN FROM SOL, so taking a prefix is an honest way to draw fewer
    stars from here (it is not a brightness order from anywhere else, and must not be treated as one)
  * 32 stars have no measured colour index; those carry B-V = 0.65 (solar). A stated 0.37%, not a
    silent fill.

FORMAT — 8,715 records x 20 bytes, little-endian, no header:
  f32 x, f32 y, f32 z   position in PARSECS, Sol at the origin, engine axes (see above), J2000/ICRS
  f32 absolute magnitude (the star's own luminosity — apparent brightness is derived, not stored)
  f32 colour index B-V

WHAT IS *NOT* IN HERE, deliberately
  Nothing derived. No temperatures, no RGB, no sizes, and no APPARENT magnitudes. B-V and absolute
  magnitude are what was MEASURED; the engine turns B-V into a temperature (Ballesteros) and a colour
  (Planck through the CIE observer), and turns absolute magnitude plus true distance into apparent
  brightness. That is physics, and physics belongs in the engine. Baking any of it into the asset
  would make the sky a picture instead of a consequence.

  No constellation lines: they are a cultural figure drawn between unrelated stars at wildly
  different distances, not a physical relationship. The stars are real; the joins would not be.
