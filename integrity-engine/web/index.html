The premise

Most engines paint a picture of physics. Integrity computes it.

There is one rule — real matter with real properties, integrated honestly through time — and everything else is a consequence. The difference shows up the moment you look closely:

the usual way — authored
  • A crate falls because a designer wrote "make crates fall."
  • A wall shatters into pieces an artist cut ahead of time.
  • A planet orbits along a path baked into a spline.
  • Sand piles at whatever slope looks about right.
Integrity — emergent
  • A crate falls because it is unsupported.
  • Rock breaks only when stress exceeds its strength.
  • Orbits, escape velocity and precession fall out of gravity alone.
  • Sand finds its angle of repose because grains stop sliding when friction holds.

The rules of the house

Three commitments, kept all the way down.

// emergent

Behavior is a consequence, never a script.

Kepler orbits, escape velocity, the angle a sand pile holds, crater scaling — all fall out of particle physics. None are coded as rules.

A dropped Moon shatters because impact energy dwarfs its binding energy — not because someone authored the pieces.
// faithful

The parameters declare real physics.

G, a planet's mass, a material's density and strength — these declare real numbers. The engine's job is to honor them exactly, not to approximate for convenience.

When the model and reality disagree, we hunt the missing physics — we never turn a dial to fake the result.
// legible

Honest physics can be re-derived by playing.

If the simulation is faithful, you can discover the laws from inside it. A child — and one day an embodied AI — could infer nature by experiment.

That's the point: a fudged engine teaches wrong physics. This one is meant to be a place you can learn from.

How honesty scales

Two layers, both honest.

Physics runs across enormous ranges of scale, so Integrity is explicit about what it resolves and what it models — and never lets the second masquerade as the first.

Resolved emergent

Real particles interacting under real forces. Nothing stands between the law and the result — the behavior is the physics playing out.

Declared modeled

For physics below the resolution, a subgrid model. But every declared model is derived from real physics, flagged as declared, and replaceable by resolving it — honesty you can audit, not a shortcut you can't see.
